History of Hampi

Krishnadevaraya's reign is considered the pinnacle of the Vijayanagara Empire's power and cultural achievements

He successfully waged wars against neighboring kingdoms, including the Bahmani Sultanate, the Gajapati Kingdom of Odisha, and various smaller principalities.

He built and repaired numerous tanks, dams, and canals to improve agricultural productivity, including the renowned Chandragiri Tank.

He supported eminent poets, including the "Ashtadiggajas" (Eight Literary Giants), such as Allasani Peddana and Tenali Ramakrishna.

One of his most notable achievements was the conquest of the Raichur Doab, a fertile region between the Krishna and Tungabhadra rivers, from the Bijapur Sultanate in 1520.

Krishnadevaraya's reign is often referred to as a "Golden Age" for Telugu literature. He was a polyglot and authored the famous work Amuktamalyada in Telugu, which offers insights into statecraft and governance.
