History of Hampi

The Vijayanagara Empire (14th to 16th century CE) is well-documented, the earlier and lesser-known history of Hampi often remains overshadowed. Evidence suggests Hampi was inhabited as early as the Neolithic period, and some believe it is the legendary Kishkindha from the Ramayana, though this connection is seldom emphasized. The Vijayanagara Empire, founded by Harihara I and Bukkaraya I, made Hampi its capital in the 14th century, but details of earlier settlements and the formation of the empire are often glossed over in historical texts. The rise of Krishnadevaraya and his cultural and military expansion, which brought Hampi to its peak, is widely acknowledged, but his efforts to revive Hindu traditions and his policies of religious tolerance towards Muslims and Jains are often understated. The Mahanavami Dibba, an important royal platform, and other structures reflect the empire's grandeur and religious harmony, but the intricate military, political strife, and internal conflicts that contributed to its decline, especially the loss of valuable archives, remain underexplored.
